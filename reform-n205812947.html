Law n°205812947

Legislative Draft – Education and Culture Section

Article 1: Integration of Local Culture in School Programs

  1. Purpose: Preserve and prioritize local culture in education, encouraging students to connect deeply with their regional identity.
  2. Regulations: Every school must dedicate a minimum of 5 hours per week to local cultural studies, including history, traditions, and regional languages.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: Schools failing to meet this quota will face a 50% reduction in government funding for extracurricular programs.

Article 2: Technology in the Classroom – Limits and Objectives

  1. Purpose: Balance the use of technology in education to enhance learning without creating digital dependency.
  2. Regulations: Each student will be allowed a maximum of 2 hours of screen time per day for educational purposes. Devices will be monitored, and misuse will result in a temporary ban of technology access for that student.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: Schools will conduct monthly audits to ensure compliance. Repeat violations by a school will lead to a 20% reduction in technology funding.

Article 3: Mandatory Artistic Disciplines in Schools

  1. Purpose: Develop creativity and appreciation for artistic expression as a key educational pillar.
  2. Regulations: All students must participate in one public art project per semester, such as murals, theater performances, or exhibitions, directly contributing to their community.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: Schools that fail to meet this requirement will be obligated to allocate 10% of their academic budget to artistic development programs.

Article 4: Relevance of Tradition and Cultural Reinterpretations

  1. Purpose: Promote the modern reinterpretation of traditional elements, enhancing the national cultural heritage through new artistic means.
  2. Regulations: Scholarships and funding will be established to create projects integrating modern reinterpretations of the country’s culinary and cultural traditions.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: Funded projects will be subject to an annual evaluation to measure their cultural impact and innovation produced.

Article 5: Restoration of Cultural Heritage

  1. Purpose: Protect and preserve the national cultural heritage, with particular attention to the resources allocated for the restoration of historical assets.
  2. Regulations: The government will establish a three-year program for the restoration of cultural assets, prioritizing national heritage sites and objects of historical importance.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: An independent commission of experts will be responsible for overseeing the restoration works and providing an annual report on the progress of the projects.



Legislative Draft – Education and Work Section

Article 7: Introduction of a Universal Minimum Wage

  1. Purpose: Guarantee economic security for all citizens while fostering meritocracy.
  2. Regulations: The universal minimum wage will be granted only to citizens who perform 8 hours per month of community service, such as cleaning public spaces or assisting local organizations. Non-compliance will result in the suspension of benefits for 3 months.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: Local governments will publish monthly transparency reports tracking community service hours and wage distributions.

Article 8: Mandatory Advanced Professional Training

  1. Purpose: Ensure citizens remain competitive in an evolving economy.
  2. Regulations: All working-age citizens must complete 3 hours of certified professional training per month to remain eligible for job opportunities in state-funded projects.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: Employers must submit employee training reports quarterly. Companies failing to comply will face a 10% corporate tax increase.

Article 9: Sustainability of the Public Health System

  1. Purpose: Ensure a public healthcare system that guarantees universal access to care while preserving the economic sustainability of the country.
  2. Regulations: Efficiency measures will be implemented in the public healthcare system to optimize resource allocation, including preventive and health education programs.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: Every year, an audit will be conducted on the healthcare system, aiming to reduce waste and improve the effectiveness of the services provided.

Article 10: Revision of Taxes on High Incomes

  1. Purpose: Review fiscal policies related to high incomes to encourage productivity and reward work efficiency.
  2. Regulations: Taxes on higher incomes will be reduced to stimulate investment in entrepreneurial initiatives and the creation of new jobs.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: Each year, a fiscal commission will be tasked with analyzing the economic effects of the tax reduction and its impact on unemployment rates and job opportunities.

Article 11: Promotion of Culinary Traditions

  1. Purpose: Promote the enhancement of culinary traditions as part of cultural heritage and economic opportunities.
  2. Regulations: Vocational training schools and universities will be encouraged to include courses that explore traditional cuisine as a tool for professional, gastronomic, and tourism growth.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: The national tourism promotion agency will initiate a certification program for restaurateurs who contribute to the enhancement of traditional cuisine.

Article 12: Restoration and Conservation of Cultural Heritage

  1. Purpose: Promote the restoration and conservation of the country’s historical and cultural heritage, facilitating access to culture for future generations.
  2. Regulations: The government will establish a national fund for the restoration of cultural assets, prioritizing historically significant national heritage sites and objects.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: An annual public report will be issued on the progress of restoration works, involving experts and international cultural institutions.

Article 13: Creation of Urban Green Spaces

  1. Purpose: Enhance the quality of life in cities by creating and expanding green spaces, thereby improving the environment and citizens’ well-being.
  2. Regulations: A percentage of the municipal budget will be required to be allocated to the expansion and maintenance of urban green spaces, encouraging community involvement in the planning process.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: Each municipality must submit an annual plan for the expansion of green areas, aiming to increase the green space per inhabitant.

Article 14: Right of Asylum for Refugees of Conflicts and Climate Change

  1. Purpose: Ensure the right of asylum for all individuals fleeing armed conflicts or climate change, promoting an inclusive and internationally solidaristic policy.
  2. Regulations: The government will ensure that every asylum seeker is treated fairly, with access to the necessary resources for integration into the country, without discrimination.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: A commission will be established to monitor asylum policies and integration, issuing an annual report on the situation and progress made.



Legislative Draft – Environment and Sustainability Section

Article 15: Limitation of Water Consumption

  1. Purpose: Promote efficient water usage to prevent resource depletion.
  2. Regulations: Each household will be allocated 10 liters of water per person per day. Exceeding this limit will result in progressive fines starting at €50 per extra liter consumed.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: Water meters will be checked weekly, and excess usage will be automatically reported to local authorities.

Article 16: Taxation for Polluting Companies

  1. Purpose: Encourage companies to reduce their environmental impact and contribute fairly to environmental protection.
  2. Regulations: Companies that produce pollution will be subject to additional taxes, with the aim of encouraging them to implement more ecological technologies and reduce their pollution emissions.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: A government agency will be responsible for monitoring the effectiveness of these policies and reviewing the tax regulations every two years based on technological and environmental developments.

Article 17: Investments in Green Infrastructure

  1. Purpose: Expand green spaces and improve urban sustainability.
  2. Regulations: All citizens must dedicate 3 hours per month to environmental activities, such as tree planting, park maintenance, or clean-up initiatives. Failure to comply will result in a €30 monthly eco-tax.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: Local environmental agencies will verify participation and publish monthly reports.

Article 18: Expansion of Urban Green Spaces

  1. Purpose: Enhance environmental quality in cities through the creation of new green spaces, benefiting the health and well-being of the population.
  2. Regulations: Tax incentives will be provided for municipalities that invest in expanding green spaces, especially in densely populated areas. Every urban plan must include a minimum quota of green space per inhabitant.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: Local authorities will be required to submit an annual report on the progress of green space expansion projects and their impact on improving air quality.

Article 19: Promotion of Environmental Sustainability

  1. Purpose: Foster individual and corporate responsibility for environmental protection.
  2. Regulations: Companies generating waste must recycle at least 80% of it or face a 15% increase in annual taxation. Citizens must sort and submit waste for recycling; failure to do so will result in weekly fines of €10.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: Local governments will implement monthly inspections of businesses and households, publicly reporting violators.



Legislative Draft – Human Rights and Justice Section

Article 20: Abolition of the Death Penalty

  1. Purpose: Uphold the dignity of human life and prevent irreversible judicial mistakes.
  2. Regulations: Any state official or institution found to impose capital punishment will face permanent dismissal and criminal prosecution.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: Human rights watchdogs will conduct annual audits of judicial sentencing.

Article 21: Right to Asylum and Refugee Protection

  1. Purpose: Provide fair treatment and support to asylum seekers.
  2. Regulations: Refugees granted asylum must complete 50 hours of cultural integration programs within their first 6 months, covering local language, laws, and work readiness. Refusal to participate will result in withdrawal of benefits.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: The refugee office will conduct quarterly checks on integration progress and publish success rates.

Article 22: Obligation of Asylum for Victims of Conflicts and Climate Change

  1. Purpose: Guarantee protection and reception for those fleeing armed conflicts or climate change, as an expression of international solidarity and respect for human rights.
  2. Regulations: Each AXIS member state commits to granting asylum to refugees from areas affected by conflict or environmental disasters related to climate change, providing them with fundamental rights to sustenance and integration.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: Each year, an independent committee monitors the implementation of asylum policies and the adequacy of resources provided to refugees to ensure their rights and well-being are respected.

Article 23: Integration of Psychological Services and Mental Health

  1. Purpose: Promote mental health awareness without unnecessary overuse of services.
  2. Regulations: Citizens requesting psychological services must attend an initial assessment and mandatory 2-hour mental health education session before receiving treatment.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: Healthcare providers must submit monthly reports verifying adherence to guidelines. Misreporting will incur a €500 fine per violation.

Article 24: Right to Privacy and Confidentiality of Information

  1. Purpose: Safeguard personal data and protect against unjustified surveillance.
  2. Regulations: Citizens must explicitly authorize any data-sharing, and companies breaching this rule will face fines of up to €1 million per incident.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: A digital privacy watchdog will audit companies every 6 months to ensure compliance.

Article 25: Reform of Fiscal Policies on High Incomes

  1. Purpose: Stimulate economic efficiency and encourage productivity through a tax system that does not excessively penalize high incomes.
  2. Regulations: Taxes on high incomes will be reduced to encourage investment in professional initiatives and business growth, while maintaining the global market’s competitiveness.
  3. Monitoring and Verification: Every year, fiscal policy will be reviewed to ensure that the reduction of taxes on high incomes does not harm the state resources allocated to welfare programs.
